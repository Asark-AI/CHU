This site summarizes the course material for Weeks 1–9 and covers CHU's information systems: system components, hardware & software requirements, networking, security, policies, and practical exercises designed for hands-on learning.

Course goals

Understand how information systems support academic operations, secure data, and enable digital learning at CHU.

Week 6 — Project Integration and Midterm Progress Review

Overview

By Week 6, Coastal Heritage University (CHU) has successfully applied various Information Systems concepts — from system components to hardware, networking, and artificial intelligence. This week focuses on integrating these systems, evaluating progress, and identifying areas for improvement to ensure smooth continuation of the project.

A. Integration of Previous Systems

AreaSystems IntegratedPurpose / Outcome
Administration Student Information System (SIS), Accounting Software, HR Management System Centralized database linking student data with finance and staff records for accuracy and consistency.
Academics Learning Management System (Moodle), Online Library, AI Grading Tools Enables e-learning, online assessments, and automated grading with immediate feedback to students.
Communication Email, Zoom, Google Workspace Streamlines communication among staff, students, and departments for better coordination.
Security & Networking Firewalls, Biometric Access, CCTV Systems Ensures campus safety, data protection, and secure access to digital platforms.
AI & IIoT Chatbots, Smart Energy Systems Enhances campus automation and efficient resource utilization.

B. Evaluation of Progress

  1. Academic Integration:
    • Online course registration and e-learning tools are fully functional.
    • Staff and students actively use Moodle and Google Classroom.
  2. Administrative Operations:
    • Payroll, HR, and accounting systems have been digitalized.
    • Financial reports can now be generated automatically.
  3. Networking and Connectivity:
    • Reliable Wi‑Fi coverage across campus.
    • LAN setup is stable, with minimal downtime reported.
  4. AI & IoT Implementation:
    • AI chatbot tested for student queries.
    • Pilot IoT devices (smart lighting and sensors) deployed in main library and ICT lab.

C. Challenges Identified

  • Limited bandwidth during peak internet usage hours.
  • Some staff require additional ICT training.
  • Incomplete integration between finance and student systems.
  • Occasional power interruptions affecting online systems.

D. Proposed Solutions

  • Upgrade network bandwidth and implement traffic management.
  • Organize ICT training workshops for lecturers and administrators.
  • Employ API integration to link all digital platforms.
  • Install backup power sources (solar/inverters) for essential systems.

E. Next Steps (Weeks 7–11)

  • Develop and test Google Forms for data collection.
  • Automate form responses and integrate with Google Sheets.
  • Design and host the university’s official website.
  • Implement JavaScript interactivity and dynamic content.

✅ Summary: Week 6 marks a key turning point in CHU’s IS project. The institution has established a strong digital foundation, integrating academic, administrative, and communication systems. The next phase will focus on automation, website development, and interactive online systems.

Week 7 — Google Forms (Foundations)

Overview

In Week 7, Coastal Heritage University (CHU) explored the use of Google Forms as a tool for data collection, feedback, and process automation. The university applied Google Forms to simplify internal operations, improve communication, and support data-driven decision-making.

A. Purpose of Using Google Forms at CHU

  • Collect structured data from students, staff, and other stakeholders.
  • Digitize manual processes such as surveys, feedback, and applications.
  • Automate data storage and analysis through Google Sheets integration.
  • Enhance transparency and accuracy in record-keeping.

B. Application Example: Student Feedback Form

Form Title: Coastal Heritage University – Course Evaluation Form

Objective: To collect feedback from students about teaching quality, course content, and overall learning experience to improve academic delivery.

  1. Personal Information – Student name, ID number, and program of study.
  2. Course Details – Course title, code, and lecturer’s name.
  3. Teaching Evaluation – Questions rating clarity, punctuality, and engagement (Likert scale: 1–5).
  4. Learning Materials – Assessment of course resources, notes, and online materials.
  5. Suggestions – Open-ended section for student comments and recommendations.

C. Other Google Forms Created at CHU

  • Job Application Form — HR
  • Staff Appraisal Form — Administration
  • Event Registration Form — Student clubs
  • Library Book Request Form — Library services
  • ICT Support Request Form — ICT Department

D. Data Collection and Analysis Process

  1. Data Collection: Forms distributed via email, website, and portal; responses recorded in Google Sheets.
  2. Data Analysis: Responses summarized into charts and tables using Google Sheets tools.
  3. Reporting: ICT and admin teams generate reports for management and share feedback with faculty.

E. Benefits of Using Google Forms in CHU

BenefitDescription
EfficiencyAutomates data collection and reduces paperwork.
AccuracyMinimizes errors by validating input and standardizing responses.
AccessibilityForms accessible on mobile, tablet, or computer.
Real-Time ReportingData updates instantly in Google Sheets for quick analysis.
Cost-EffectiveFree tool that saves printing and administrative costs.
Environmental SustainabilityReduces paper usage, supporting CHU’s green initiative.

Week 8 — Google Forms Add-Ons & Automation

Overview

In Week 8, CHU advanced its use of Google Forms by integrating add-ons and automation tools to improve workflow efficiency and data management. These integrations transform submissions into automated workflows that send emails, generate charts, and update databases.

A. Purpose of Automation

  • Save time by eliminating repetitive tasks.
  • Improve accuracy through consistent data processing.
  • Enhance communication via automatic notifications and responses.
  • Support quick decision-making with real-time visualization.

B. Add-Ons and Tools Used at CHU

Add-On / ToolFunctionExample of Use in CHU
Form NotificationsSends automatic email alerts after submission.Students receive confirmation emails after submitting course evaluations.
Form PublisherConverts responses into formatted documents (PDF/Google Docs).Generates staff appraisal reports for HR.
FormLimiterCloses the form after a set date or response limit.Closes job application forms after the deadline or 100 submissions.
Google Sheets IntegrationLinks responses for real-time analysis.Instant charts summarizing student feedback.
AutocratMerges responses into templates and sends personalized documents.Generates certificates for workshop participants.
Form MuleSends personalized email responses based on answers.Sends customized feedback to students.

C. Example: Automated Course Evaluation Process

  1. Students complete the CHU Course Evaluation Form.
  2. Responses are sent to Google Sheets.
  3. Form Publisher generates a report for each lecturer.
  4. Form Notifications sends an alert to the Quality Assurance Office.
  5. The report is shared with the Head of Department automatically.

D. Impact of Automation at CHU

AreaImpact / Improvement
Administrative EfficiencyReduces workload and speeds up form processing.
Data AccuracyAutomated flow minimizes human errors.
Decision-MakingReal-time charts enhance planning and reviews.
TransparencyAutomated reports prevent data manipulation.
User SatisfactionInstant confirmations and updates for users.

E. Challenges and Solutions

ChallengeProposed Solution
Staff unfamiliar with add-onsConduct regular ICT training workshops.
Internet downtimeInstall backup routers and increase bandwidth.
Storage limitsUpgrade to Google Workspace for Education.

✅ Summary: In Week 8, CHU automated Google Forms workflows using add-ons such as Form Publisher and Form Notifications, resulting in faster, more accurate, and more transparent administrative processes.
