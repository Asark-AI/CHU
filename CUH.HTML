Week 6 — Project Integration and Midterm Progress Review

Overview

By Week 6, Coastal Heritage University (CHU) has successfully applied various Information Systems concepts — from system components to hardware, networking, and artificial intelligence. This week focuses on integrating these systems, evaluating progress, and identifying areas for improvement to ensure smooth continuation of the project.

  1. Academic Integration:
    • Online course registration and e-learning tools are fully functional.

Week 7 — Google Forms (Foundations)

Overview

In Week 7, Coastal Heritage University (CHU) explored the use of Google Forms as a tool for data collection, feedback, and process automation. The university applied Google Forms to simplify internal operations, improve communication, and support data-driven decision-making.

A. Purpose of Using Google Forms at CHU

  • Collect structured data from students, staff, and other stakeholders.
  • Digitize manual processes such as surveys, feedback, and applications.
  • Automate data storage and analysis through Google Sheets integration.
  • Enhance transparency and accuracy in record-keeping.

B. Application Example: Student Feedback Form

Form Title: Coastal Heritage University – Course Evaluation Form

Objective: To collect feedback from students about teaching quality, course content, and overall learning experience to improve academic delivery.

  1. Personal Information – Student name, ID number, and program of study.
  2. Course Details – Course title, code, and lecturer’s name.
  3. Teaching Evaluation – Questions rating clarity, punctuality, and engagement (Likert scale: 1–5).
  4. Learning Materials – Assessment of course resources, notes, and online materials.
  5. Suggestions – Open-ended section for student comments and recommendations.

C. Other Google Forms Created at CHU

  • Job Application Form — HR
  • Staff Appraisal Form — Administration
  • Event Registration Form — Student clubs
  • Library Book Request Form — Library services
  • ICT Support Request Form — ICT Department

D. Data Collection and Analysis Process

  1. Data Collection: Forms distributed via email, website, and portal; responses recorded in Google Sheets.
  2. Data Analysis: Responses summarized into charts and tables using Google Sheets tools.
  3. Reporting: ICT and admin teams generate reports for management and share feedback with faculty.

Week 5 — Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT)

Overview

Coastal Heritage University (CHU) embraces Artificial Intelligence (AI) and the Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) to enhance academic delivery, campus management, and administrative efficiency. These technologies promote automation, predictive analytics, and intelligent decision-making across the university.

A. Applications of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in CHU

AI ApplicationDescriptionExample of Use at CHU
AI Chatbot for Student Support Provides automated assistance to students and staff. The CHU Virtual Assistant answers FAQs about admissions, timetables, fees, and academic regulations 24/7.
Predictive Analytics for Academic Performance Uses historical data to predict student outcomes. AI analyzes students’ grades and attendance to identify those at risk of failing and alerts academic advisors.
Facial Recognition for Attendance Automates attendance marking for lectures. Cameras with AI software record student presence during classes and store data securely in the university database.
Smart Grading System Automatically grades multiple-choice assessments and analyzes results. AI tools in the Learning Management System (LMS) provide instant feedback to students after online quizzes.
Research Assistance Tools Uses AI to improve research efficiency. Tools like Grammarly, ChatGPT, and Turnitin AI detection help lecturers and students with writing, editing, and originality checks.

B. Applications of the Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) in CHU

IIoT ApplicationDescriptionExample of Use at CHU
Smart Energy Management Monitors and controls electricity usage in real time. Sensors installed in lecture halls and offices automatically turn off lights and air conditioners when rooms are empty.
Smart Security System Connects security devices to the central monitoring system. CCTV cameras, motion detectors, and biometric locks are integrated to enhance campus safety.
Smart Classrooms Combines IoT devices and internet connectivity to enhance learning. Interactive whiteboards and digital projectors connected to campus Wi‑Fi support hybrid and online learning.
Equipment Maintenance Alerts Predicts maintenance needs for IT and laboratory equipment. Sensors detect equipment faults and notify the ICT department for timely repairs.
Environmental Monitoring System Tracks temperature, humidity, and air quality in laboratories. IoT sensors send real-time data to facility managers for safe and comfortable working conditions.

C. How AI and IIoT Enhance Decision-Making in CHU

  1. Data-Driven Insights: AI systems analyze large volumes of academic and administrative data to generate actionable reports for management.
  2. Predictive Decision-Making: Predictive analytics identify future trends in enrollment, performance, and resource demand.
  3. Real-Time Monitoring: IIoT devices provide instant data on energy use, security, and campus infrastructure, helping leaders make faster decisions.
  4. Operational Efficiency: Automation of routine tasks allows staff to focus on strategic activities.
  5. Improved Student Experience: AI chatbots and smart systems provide quick responses and personalized learning support.

D. Benefits of AI and IIoT Integration at CHU

  • Reduced operational costs through automation
  • Enhanced data security and accuracy
  • Improved teaching and learning experiences
  • Efficient campus management
  • Early detection of academic or technical problems
  • Real-time decision support for administrators

✅ Summary: Artificial Intelligence and IIoT are transforming Coastal Heritage University into a smart, technology-driven campus. Through intelligent automation, predictive analytics, and connected devices, CHU enhances academic excellence, operational efficiency, and sustainable development.

Week 4 — Information Systems for Business and Development

Overview

Coastal Heritage University (CHU) uses various types of Information Systems (IS) to manage its daily operations, support decision-making, and improve academic and administrative efficiency. Each system contributes to achieving the university’s goal of providing quality education and effective institutional management.

A. Types of Information Systems Used in CHU

Type of SystemDescriptionExample / Use in CHU
Transaction Processing System (TPS) Handles routine and repetitive transactions that occur daily. The Student Information System (SIS) processes student admissions, fee payments, course registrations, and exam records.
Management Information System (MIS) Collects and summarizes data from TPS for managerial decision-making. The Academic and Administrative Dashboard provides reports on student enrollment, staff performance, and financial summaries for management.
Decision Support System (DSS) Assists top management in making informed and data-driven decisions. A performance analytics tool helps the university evaluate academic trends, staff productivity, and budget allocation.
Executive Information System (EIS) Provides senior executives with easy access to internal and external information relevant to strategic goals. The Vice-Chancellor’s Portal displays summarized reports on university performance, enrollment growth, and expenditure patterns.
Knowledge Work System (KWS) Supports professional knowledge creation and sharing. The Research Repository System allows lecturers and researchers to upload, access, and share academic publications and research findings.
Office Automation System (OAS) Automates day-to-day office tasks to improve productivity. Tools like Microsoft Office 365, Google Workspace, and document management software are used for word processing, emailing, and file sharing.
Expert System (ES) Mimics human expertise to solve specific problems or provide advice. An academic advising chatbot helps students choose courses based on their program requirements and grades.

B. Role of Information Systems in CHU’s Development

  1. Enhancing Efficiency
    • Automated systems reduce paperwork and minimize human error.
    • Real-time access to student and staff information improves workflow.
  2. Supporting Decision-Making
    • Managers and faculty can analyze academic data to make informed decisions.
    • Predictive analysis helps anticipate student performance trends.
  3. Promoting Transparency and Accountability
    • Automated systems provide digital records that ensure data integrity and reduce corruption.
  4. Improving Communication
    • Email systems, online portals, and collaborative tools connect students, lecturers, and administrators effectively.
  5. Facilitating Research and Innovation
    • Online databases and knowledge-sharing systems encourage academic collaboration.
  6. Driving Institutional Growth
    • Information Systems improve service delivery, attract more students, and enhance CHU’s reputation as a technology-driven university.

C. Example Integration

Scenario: When a student registers online, the Transaction Processing System (TPS) records the registration. Data from TPS is analyzed by the MIS to produce reports for the registrar. The DSS then uses that data to predict course demand and recommend timetable adjustments. Finally, the EIS summarizes the results for university management to plan resource allocation.

This integration demonstrates how different IS types work together to support development and operational excellence at CHU.

✅ Summary: Information Systems at Coastal Heritage University form the backbone of modern academic and administrative management. By implementing TPS, MIS, DSS, EIS, KWS, OAS, and Expert Systems, the university enhances productivity, decision-making, and overall institutional growth.

Week 3 — Networking and Communication

Overview

CHU uses a robust LAN, Wi‑Fi, and internet connectivity to connect departments, lecture halls, and hostels.

A. Network Structure Design

Key components include LAN, Wi‑Fi, WAN links, central servers, routers & switches. A simple diagram (conceptual) links servers → routers/switches → access points & endpoints.

B. Network Topology

CHU adopts a Star Topology for easy management and high reliability.

C. Communication Systems

  • Email (Gmail/Outlook) for official communication
  • Instant messaging (Teams/WhatsApp) for coordination
  • Video conferencing (Zoom/Meet) for virtual lectures
  • University portal & website for public info and e-learning

D. Network Security Measures

  1. Password protection and strong password policies
  2. Firewalls and antivirus software
  3. Encryption for sensitive transmissions
  4. Regular backups and access control
  5. Network monitoring by IT officers

E. ICT Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) — Key points

  • ICT resources for academic/administrative use only
  • No sharing of passwords; no unauthorized software installs
  • Report security incidents immediately to ICT

✅ Summary: Secure, fast, and reliable network design ensures CHU's academic efficiency and ethical ICT usage.
